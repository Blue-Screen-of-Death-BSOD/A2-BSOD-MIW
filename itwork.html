IT Interview

Clapp questions

1. What kind of work is done by the IT professional?

The IT professional works around communication and business systems to help bring corporate strategies to fruition. The value of this is that the professional can convert the corporate strategy to natural management of employees in the IT department of the organization. Bridging these two groups creates efficiency on a human level reflected on an IT system level through execution.


2. What kinds of people does the IT professional interact with? Are they other IT professionals? Clients? Investors? The general public?

The professional interacts with other stakeholders in the business, such as employees and corporate management, but does not necessarily interact with customers or clients. The professional does interact with software providers for licensing purposes and to negotiate terms. However, the general public is usually not included in any of these groups directly. Indirectly, the professional’s management of the company’s services helps the available public customers.


3. Where does the IT professional spend most of their time?

The IT professional spends most of their time in online meetings (due to covid procedures) managing staff and discussing essential decisions such as staff allocation and business strategies.


4. What aspect of their position is most challenging?

The IT professional needs to balance multiple stakeholders' priorities without compromising on one. One example is thats: customer service needs to have an efficient system that meets the same supply of staff consumers demands. If demand is too high, the system could break down or require more staffing to meet the consumer stakeholder priorities. However, this doesn't help the business' efficiency of minimizing costs. One solution to meet surges in service demand and prices is implementing an automated IVR system that handles basic inquiries without human operators.

Transcript

1. Please tell us about your IT profession. What exactly do you do?

My role is IT Portfolio and Planning Manager, responsibilities of my role are to estimate, prioritize, and allocate all incoming work that requires support from IT. Forecast the number of IT resources needed to support upcoming work. Report on resource contention and adherence to schedules and overall portfolio health. Reconcile allocation of costs with our finance teams. Define processes, patterns, and document templates for the IT Team to use to ensure consistency of delivery.


2. What part of the position is most challenging?

I balance multiple stakeholders' priorities, such as the customer service department's need for more efficient inbound phone traffic while preventing too many staff.


3. Would you please tell us about the industry you work in?

My company operates in the retail sector with a focus on home office supplies. The company has an online presence that manages sales and orders for both customers and staff. Also, the industry requires a customer service presence beyond retail staff to respond to customer inquiries.


4. What other kinds of work do you have to do?

Develop IT tools to support my team's function (ITBM), recruit, train, and develop my team, Organise social events to support organizational engagement, facilitate training sessions with IT and business stakeholders.


5. Who are all the different people you interact with within your work? Would you please tell us about them?

Finance teams supply cost centers so we can budget against them, Human Resources support recruitment, training, and Team development, IT developers provide estimates for work, and full tech builds, project managers, assist with planning schedules and managing team members, business stakeholders provide business requirements and direction on the priority of work.


6. Do you interact with clients and investors?

I don’t interact with clients or investors in my role, but I do engage with external vendors to purchase software applications that support our business. I mainly interact with other colleagues within the company across different departments.


7. What aspects of your work do you spend the most time on? Please tell us about these.

I am estimating new IT work and planning Team Member's allocations to meet the needs of the business. This is an important use of my time as it allows for the reallocation of staff when business needs to adapt to change. To do this, I spend most of my time in meetings with different employees and managers.


8. Finally, can you share an example of the work you do that best captures the essence of the IT industry?

The work I do involves coordinating the capabilities of many different skills from both IT and business areas. The essence of the IT industry today is aligning these individuals in cohesive teams to achieve focused outcomes.

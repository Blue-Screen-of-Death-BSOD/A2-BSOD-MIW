Reflection

Group

Throughout assignment 2, our group (Blue screen of death) exceeded delegating tasks between the group members, so every person had their role. On top of that, each group member was good at completing their task to the group's timelines for each other. For the meetings that were conducted, members of the group were good at being punctual and showing up on time, and during meetings, communication flowed easily, and we were all able to collaborate productively and effectively on ideas quickly, and there was no awkward silence. There was a large amount of respect between group members when collaborating on ideas which created a positive environment that helped encourage further collaboration. The group was well organized when it came to google docs and team for collaboration. The group was good at creating and organizing google docs for the report and each meeting, so each member had a good point of reference for what they needed to have completed for that week. We were also scheduling meetings on Microsoft teams, so it was easy to see when the next meeting was taking place.

Although our communication in meetings and calls went well, our communication outside calls via messenger/Microsoft teams was not so well with responses taking a while to be read and replied to. There was also a substantial lack of communication at the beginning of assignment 2 (however, it steadily improved throughout the weeks of completing assignment 2). With this being said, there could have been much improvement in these areas in the form of perhaps more meetings to be done earlier in the assignment and maybe a fixed time where everyone can see/read/reply to messages during the day. Another area which could have seen some improvements is working more together on certain parts, this way the work would be proofread by one or more members of the group and the quality of the work would be increased. An improvement of communication outside of calls could have improved our teamwork on specific tasks. Another way this could be improved could have been by doing proportions of work together rather than a whole part individually (this is harder to implement, though, and it is much easier to delegate work evenly if it is done individually).
